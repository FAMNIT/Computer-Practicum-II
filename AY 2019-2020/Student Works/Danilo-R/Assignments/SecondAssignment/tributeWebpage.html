Walt Disney

The man behind the magic


The timeline of Walt Disney's life:

  • 1901 - Born in Chicago, USA

  • 1911 - Moves to Kansas City, MO

  • 1917 - Moves to Chicago, where he draws pictures for the McKinley High School newspaper and attends evening classes at the Chicago Academy of Fine Arts, hoping to become a newspaper cartoonist

  • 1920 - Meets Ub Iwerks and forms Iwerks-Disney Commercial Artists. The company fails after one month. Walt and Iwerks get jobs with the Kansas City Slide Company and discover animation. While keeping his day job, Walt creates Newman Laugh-O-grams, which produces ads and topical shorts and story cartoons

  • 1923 - Laugh-O-Gram goes bankrupt. Walt moves to Hollywood to become a director. With his brother Roy, he stablishes the Disney Brother Studio when he lands a contract for the "Alice Comedies", a series in which a young girl filmed in live action interacts with animated characters

  • 1928 - Walt creates Mickey Mouse. He joins the vanguard of the talking-picture revolution when he produces Steamboat Willie, an innovative cartoon that syncrhonizes sound and animation. During next year, Mickey becomes a national sensation

  • 1932 - Acquires exclusive use of three-strip Technicolor for cartoons and incorporates the technology in his films. He hires teachers from thee Chouinard Art Institute to give classes at the Studio

  • 1939 - Wins an honorary Academy Award with a unique casting: one full-sized Oscar and seven minutes miniatures for Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs

  • 1946 - Walt, who has provided Mickey Mouse's voice for nearly 20 years, reassigns the role for Fun and Fancy Free. The studio releases Song of the South, which enjoys commercial business

  • 1948 - Visits a railroad fair in Chicago and soon decides to create a railroad for his home

  • 1950 - Cinderella becomes Disney's first commercially successful animated feature since Snow white and the Seven Dwarf. Disney releases Treasure Island, its first all live-action movie

  • 1954 - Disney Contracts with ABC television to produce a one-hour television progrma in exchange for a $500,000 investment by ABC in Disneyland. Walt buys 244 acres of land near Anaheim, CA to be the site for Disneyland

  • 1960 - Disney Serves as Head of pageantry of the 1960 Winter Olympics

  • 1961 - Walt and Roy establish plans to create the California Institute of the Arts through the merger of two financially distressed professional schools, the Los Angeles Conservatory of Music, founded in 1883, and the Chouinard Art Institute, founded in 1921

  • 1965 - Walt Disney Studios purchases land in Orlando, FL for EPCOT, the Experimental Prototype Community of Tomorrow, a foreshadowing of the new Urbanism movement, which also leads to the creation of Walt Disney World

  • 1966 - Dies on December 15

Around here, we don't look backwards for very long ... We keep moving forward, opening up new doors and doing new things because we're curious... and curiosity keeps leading us down new paths.

-- Walt Disney
