Dr.Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives




Here is a time line of Dr.Borlaug's life:



  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa

  • 1933 - Left his family's farm to attend University of Minnesota

  • 1942 - Receives a Ph.D. in Genetics and Pathology

  • 1953 - Crosses a short, sturdy dwarf breed of wheat with a high-yeidling American breed, creating a strain that responds well to fertilizer. It goes on to provide 95% of Mexico's wheat.

  • 1962 - Brings his high-yielding strains to Delhi in order to mitigate mass starvation in Indian subcontinent

  • 1970 - Receives the Nobel Peace Prize

  • 1983 - Helps seven African countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields

  • 2009 - Dies at the age of 95

"Man's survival, from the time of Adam and Eve until the invention of agriculture, must have been precarious because of his inability to ensure his food supply."
--Dr. Norman Borlaug
